La Canzone Trelingue

Mother sang it, whispering. The Cultural Revolution forbade anything foreign in China. She was five, the world was closed. The song was from a faraway country named YiDaLi, and it was a secret just like her violin. '…beautiful…serene…love…' She was transported to another world. The Red Guards discovered the violin. Then her parents died in prison. She lived with Auntie. Years passed. When the breeze rustles the leaves, she thinks she hears the song, but the melody and the words are lost. Then there's COVID. Recalling the last time when the world was closed, she studies Italian. She goes to Italy in search of…What? She doesn't know why she comes to Sorrento only that the unfamiliar air, colors and voices seem familiar. She goes to sea with a fisherman. The sun reflects on the water like scattered gems. — Sir, is it forbidden to dive from the boat? — Yes, it's the law. But, well, you're in Italy. She dives, surfaces, and floats on her back. She sings to the wide-open world: Look at the sea, so beautiful / It inspires so many emotions
